dispatched nightly stamped Friday's data with a Saturday's date. It could not be recorded, because
by the time PD0 was built production had self-healed (Monday's edition landed and superseded it).

So it is rebuilt from the healthy recording beside it by substituting back the strings the outage
is DOCUMENTED to have shown — every one of them sourced, none invented:

  masthead      'Saturday, July 11, 2026'                          POLISH-AND-DEPTH-PLAN 1.2 (surface table)
  strip         'Data through Sat Jul 11 close · pipeline ran 15:33 ET'   plan 1.2; 15:33 ET is the run's real finishedAt
  breadth       '63% above the 50-day average · at Sat's close'     plan 1.1
  index levels  ETF closes under index names, each labelled          plan 1.3 #1 (the N1 fallback, working on garbage input)
  mood gauge    'Insufficient inputs tonight — missing: …'           plan 1.3 #1 / the user's 10:09am screenshot

This exists so the instrument can be proven against the exact outage that commissioned it. A
checker that has never seen the disease is decoration (ruling E10). The repo's standing rule, from
N3: a fixture that was not recorded must say so in its own filename.
