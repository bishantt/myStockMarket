The words an app uses around a number quietly tell you what to do with it. This app uses a small, fixed vocabulary, chosen so that no word ever promises more than the data supports.

## Tendencies, not predictions

The single most important word here is **tendency**. A base rate is a tendency: a description of what has happened before, not a claim about what will happen next. Every setup card carries the line "a tendency, not a prediction" for exactly this reason. The app has one unbreakable rule — it makes no directional forecasts, ever — and the language is built to keep that rule visible.

So you will never read "this is likely to rise" or a buy/sell score. You will read how often, in the past, the price was higher afterward, and how uncertain that count is.

## The tier words are observational

When a base rate is strong enough to mention, the app labels it with a **tendency tier**, and each tier is a phrase, not a verb:

- **weak** — "watch only"
- **moderate** — "note it; check the weakeners"
- **strong** — "worth a closer look"

Notice that even the best tier says "a closer look", not "buy". These words describe the strength of the historical tendency; they never tell you to act. That is deliberate: the decision is yours, and the app's job is to give you an honest reading, not a nudge.

## Evidence grades and folklore

Alongside the tier sits an **evidence grade** — how much the published research actually supports the pattern, drawn from the app's research ledger. A pattern can look fine in the recent data and still carry a **weak** grade because the wider literature never held up. And a pattern with no real support is labelled plainly: **folklore**. The word is not an insult; it is information. "Gaps always continue, never fill" is trading-desk lore, and the app says so rather than dressing it up.

Read the tier and the grade together. A strong recent tier with a folklore grade is a warning, not a green light.
