The US trading day has a shape, and the shape matters more than most beginners expect. All times here are Eastern (ET), because that is market time and the app displays in ET only.

## The regular session

The **regular session** runs from **9:30am to 4:00pm ET** on every weekday the market is open. This is the window nearly all volume, nearly all price discovery, and nearly all of the app's data belong to. When the app talks about a daily close or a day's range, it means this session.

## Pre-market and after-hours

Trading does not stop dead at the edges of the regular session. Two extended windows bracket it:

- **Pre-market** — trading before 9:30am ET, commonly from around 4:00am, though the busy part is the hour or so before the open.
- **After-hours** — trading after the 4:00pm ET close, running into the evening.

These sessions are real, and news often breaks inside them — an earnings report at 4:05pm, an economic print before the open. But they are thin.

## Why the edges are dangerous

In pre-market and after-hours, far fewer people are trading. Thin participation has two consequences the app wants a reader to feel:

- **Wide spreads.** With few orders posted, the gap between the best price to buy and the best price to sell widens. Crossing that gap is a real cost, and it is largest exactly when the fewest people are around.
- **Jumpy prices.** With little depth, a single modest order can move the quote further than it would in daylight. A dramatic-looking after-hours move can partly reverse once the full crowd returns at 9:30am.

So an extended-hours price is a weaker signal than a regular-session price. The next lesson, on order types and the spread at `/academy/order-types-and-the-spread`, shows how to avoid paying for that thinness.

## The open and the close

Within the regular session, two windows stand out.

- **The opening 30 minutes.** The first half hour after 9:30am is the busiest and most volatile stretch of the day. Overnight news is being absorbed and spreads are still relatively wide. It is where the day's story starts, and where hasty orders are most expensive.
- **The closing auction.** At 4:00pm ET the exchange crosses a single auction print, and it is enormous — the closing auction routinely concentrates **more than 10% of the whole day's volume** into that one moment. That makes it the **deepest, most liquid window of the day**, which is why so many funds and index trades choose to transact there.

Knowing the shape — jumpy edges, a wide-open opening, a deep close — is the groundwork for trading at sensible cost rather than fighting the day's own rhythm.
