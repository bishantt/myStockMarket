Part 8

Implementation roadmap

Seven phases, each shippable and each delivering daily value before the next begins. Three sequencing decisions are deliberate: the signal log starts recording in Phase 1 (so the track record is long by the time its page ships); setup cards never ship before their accountability surface (the resolver and a visible resolved log land in the same phase); and paper trading arrives after the Academy exists, gated by the risk module — watch first, learn risk, then size paper positions.

Phases with acceptance criteria — “done” is testable, not vibes

Five consecutive nights of briefings in which every number/ticker/date passes the gate; a seeded fake number in a test draft is caught and blocked; the batch-cutoff fallback drill exercised once; publish confirmed atomic (no mixed-generation reads under a mid-publish request).
P4 · Setup cards, base rates & accountability
~2 weeks		 First pattern detectors (MA cross, 52-week-high proximity, gap-with-catalyst, RSI extreme, unusual volume, breadth states) writing signal_events history; base_rate_stat with Wilson CIs, regime slices, and the decay-stamp metadata (publication_year, evidence_grade, decay_note); vol_band computation (5/10/20-day empirical quantiles) and fan-chart render; lottery-risk flags (high-skew, low-price) computed in the scan stage; setup-card UI with icon array, reference-class chip, weakeners, baseline-comparison line; display-precision rules; evidence-grade labels and visible criteria on scan presets; the nightly resolver (signal_resolution) and a minimal track-record page (plain resolved log) — cards do not ship without them; if/then branch base rates attach, with the N<30 suppression rendering. 2–3 weeks (content-heavy)		 MDX lesson framework; ~25 launch lessons across M0–M6 (grow toward ~60), M1 anchored on US market mechanics and session structure; glossary popovers (first-occurrence discipline); worked-example drawer with on-chart annotations; Leitner review queue (max 5/day); bidirectional links with return rails; risk-module soft gate. Standing reminders while building

The Research Report’s Part 8 commitments function as the PR checklist: no directional forecasts, no bare percentages, no trending surfaces, mechanical voice, folklore labeled. When a tempting feature conflicts with the evidence chapters, the evidence chapters win — that is what they are for. TDD applies throughout (indicators, base rates, the verification gate, display rules, the sizing cap); the acceptance criteria above are deliberately phrased as tests.

Part 9

Risks & mitigations

Ordered by expected pain. The catastrophic ones are operational silence and quiet dishonesty — both engineered against, not hoped against.

RiskSeverityMitigation
Silently dead pipeline — schedules can be auto-disabled (documented for public repos after 60 idle days; unverified for private), delayed, or occasionally dropped; cron failures page nobody by default; the app would quietly serve stale data High Zero-cost heartbeat step removes the auto-disable question; failure e-mails catch crashes; the dead-man check runs on the pipeline’s own cron schedule (no weekend false alarms) with the holiday path pinging success — absence of success alerts by ~9:10pm EDT / 8:10pm EST, the same evening. Freshness timestamp rendered on the Desk; workflow_dispatch is the manual recovery.
GitHub Actions free-minutes exhaustion mid-month would kill the cron exactly like a dead pipeline Medium Designed out: the two-job split (~50 min/night ≈ 1,050/mo) leaves ~45% headroom of the 2,000 free minutes for reruns and backfills; a blocking single-job design (~1,650/mo) was explicitly rejected. If usage ever creeps, the Hetzner escape hatch is pre-priced.
Oracle drift — the product slowly grows prediction features under enthusiasm pressure Medium The non-negotiables box (Part 1) + Research Report Part 8 as the standing PR checklist; the track-record page makes any drift publicly visible in the app’s own Brier score.
Indicator or base-rate math is simply wrong — hand-rolled code feeding every displayed number Medium Unit tests against published reference values; one-time cross-check against TA-Lib in CI; the one-bar-shift lookahead guard has its own failing-test proof; Wilson CI implementation tested against textbook cases.
Supabase free pauses after 7 idle days if the pipeline itself is broken — one bug becomes two outages Medium Dead-man alert surfaces a broken pipeline the same evening, not seven days later; if the coupling still rankles, Neon free has no pause concept and Prisma makes the swap a connection-string change.
Free-tier rug-pulls — precedent is real (Alpha Vantage 500→25 req/day; Finnhub removed free candles) Medium Repository-pattern adapters make any provider swap a one-module change; the upgrade ladder is pre-researched with verified prices so the response is a same-week migration.
LLM hallucination in the briefing — a fabricated number read before the open could influence a real decision Medium Three-layer defense: LLM never computes (numbers passed in, used verbatim); per-claim source-ID citations enforced by schema; deterministic tolerance-based gate blocks unsourced numbers/tickers/dates. Residual ~0.7–1.5% grounded-hallucination rate contained by inline citations in the UI.
Alpaca data quality — IEX-only quotes differ slightly from the consolidated tape, and 7 years of history may be short for some base rates Medium For EOD closes the divergence is minor; monthly sample cross-check against Stooq/yfinance; Stooq backfill can extend depth. If quality or depth becomes the binding pain, Tiingo Power ($30, 30+ yrs, best cleanliness reputation) or EODHD ($19.99) is the targeted fix.
Scope creep / solo-maintainer fatigue — the Academy alone could swallow months Medium Phase gates with testable acceptance criteria; the widget→phase traceability table keeps design and build reconciled; P0–P3 delivers the daily ritual early so motivation compounds.
Licensing violation by accident — sharing the dashboard breaches every provider’s personal-use terms Low Basic Auth always on; preview deployments protected; FRED attribution and TradingView logo rendered; no public screenshots with data.
Batch API latency blows the evening deadline Low Job B collects ~65 minutes after submission (typical completion is well under an hour); if incomplete, cancel and extract the remainder synchronously (~3 min, ~$0.18 extra; already-processed items still bill). Worst-case publish ~00:50 UTC = 8:50pm EDT / 7:50pm EST — inside the 9:00pm ET promise year-round.
US DST edges — the close shifts between 20:00 and 21:00 UTC across the year, and the user’s own clock shifts with it Low Designed out: every trigger is UTC-fixed and sits after the close in both regimes, so no cron is ever edited; the only visible effect is that listed local times read an hour earlier on the winter clock (the schedule shows both); exchange_calendars handles US holidays and half-days; lib/time.ts owns EDT/EST labeling and is tested across both transitions.
Unverified numbers in this blueprint — Hetzner ~$5, R2 10GB free, healthchecks.io free tier, FMP month-to-month rates, Highcharts commercial range, the 60-day rule on private repos Low All flagged inline where used; none is load-bearing for the $0 baseline (each has a same-tier substitute). Confirm at signup before depending on any.

9.1  Decision changers

Three futures would legitimately reopen parts of this blueprint. Intraday trading: minute bars multiply storage ~400×, demand real-time infrastructure (SIP entitlements, a relay for client-side quotes) and a different risk posture — a different product; the Research Report’s evidence argues against wanting it. Live brokerage integration: fully available to a US resident, so the gate is earned rather than legal — order routing, credential security, and a changed risk posture arrive with it; legitimate only after the paper track record demonstrates readiness, with every friction mechanism carried over. More users than you: every data license, the auth model, and Vercel Hobby terms break simultaneously — that is a commercial product with data-redistribution contracts, not an upgrade.
